My First Naukri Boolean Tip: Using Quotes Effectively

Published on: September 29, 2025

Welcome to the first post on the Boolean Builder blog! Today, we'll talk about a fundamental aspect of crafting powerful Boolean search queries on Naukri: the importance of using quotation marks.

Why Quotes Matter

When you search for multiple words without quotes, Naukri (and most search engines) treats them as individual keywords, often returning results where the words appear separately, or in any order. For example, searching for `project manager` might return profiles that mention "project" in one section and "manager" in another, but not necessarily "project manager" as a specific phrase.

How to Use Them

By enclosing a phrase in double quotation marks (e.g., `"Project Manager"`), you tell the search engine to look for that exact phrase. This significantly improves the relevance of your search results.

Example:

  • Without quotes: `project manager` (broad, less precise)
  • With quotes: `"Project Manager"` (exact phrase, highly precise)

This simple tip can drastically improve your sourcing accuracy on Naukri. Stay tuned for next week's tip!
